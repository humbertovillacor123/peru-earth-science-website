Peru: Earth Processes & Geography

Peru is one of the most geologically fascinating countries in the world. Its mountains, earthquakes, volcanoes, and mineral resources all connect to the tectonic forces shaping South America along the Pacific margin.

Section 02

Plate Tectonics

Peru lies along a convergent plate boundary. The oceanic Nazca Plate moves beneath the South American Plate near the Peru Chile Trench.

Andean relief created by tectonic uplift

Peru’s mountain landscapes are tied to long term uplift caused by subduction.

Boundary type

Peru is near a convergent plate boundary where the Nazca Plate is subducted below the South American Plate.

Why it matters

This plate interaction explains much of Peru’s geology, including mountains, earthquakes, volcanoes, and mineral deposits.

Natural hazards

Peru can experience strong earthquakes, landslides, volcanic eruptions, and tsunamis related to this tectonic setting.

1

The plates converge

The Nazca Plate moves toward the South American Plate from the Pacific Ocean.

2

Subduction begins

The oceanic plate bends downward at the trench and sinks below the continent.

3

The Andes rise

Compression and uplift over millions of years help build the mountains of Peru.

4

Magma and volcanism

Subduction contributes to melting at depth, which supports volcanic systems in southern Peru.

Section 03

Earthquakes

Peru experiences large earthquakes because stress builds where the Nazca and South American plates interact.

🌊

Ancash earthquake, 1970

7.9 Mw

This earthquake triggered a catastrophic landslide that buried Yungay and became one of the deadliest natural disasters in Peruvian history.

🏚️

Pisco earthquake, 2007

8.0 Mw

This earthquake struck the central coast of Peru and caused major damage in Ica and surrounding areas.

📡

Preparedness today

Monitoring + drills

Peru promotes seismic monitoring, emergency drills, and public risk communication through national institutions.

Section 04

Volcanoes

Peru has important volcanoes, especially in the southern Andes. Many are composite volcanoes built by repeated layers of lava and ash.

Why volcanoes matter in Peru

Volcanoes can create fertile soils and support tourism, but they can also be dangerous because of ash fall, gas emissions, and lahars.

Positive effects

Volcanic regions may have fertile soils, strong tourism appeal, and dramatic landscapes.

Hazards

Ash, gas, falling rocks, and lahars can affect public health, agriculture, roads, and aviation.

Monitoring

The Geophysical Institute of Peru monitors active and potentially active volcanoes through its volcanic network.

Section 05

Mountains and the Andes

The Andes strongly shape Peru’s weather, water supply, biodiversity, transportation, and culture.

How the Andes affect Peru

The Andes influence rainfall patterns, temperatures, and river systems. They also store water in snow and glaciers and historically made travel more difficult.
